## Autonomous Camera-Person



Shot 1A:	[Narrator standing on a TED talk stage like they’re giving a TED talk] What's the difference between a TEDTalk, and a university lecture? 

B Roll 1:	[show a cinematic shot of a TEDtalk with cool audio, and then whoosh to a boring, poor quality video (over-exposed, low resolution, or bad color correction) of a professor giving a lecture, with poor quality audio and crickets]

Shot 1B:	[back to narrator on stage] Aside from the subject content, the only other difference would be the production value.

B Roll 2:	[pan around to multiple sliding shots of a camera operator moving a camera, director talking into a headset at a director booth, and stage managers talking to talent] {Voiceover} Ted Talks use professional videographers, lighting designers, stage management, robo-cam operators, and a director, which are all paid according to their high reliability and skill within their roles.

Shot 2A:	[green screen fades away to narrator standing in a lecture hall, with poor quality video/over exposed like before] Is there a way we can mimic the production value of a TEDtalk within an academic setting, while not blowing the budget on a production team?

Shot 3A: 	[bluey in use, filming a professor while doing a slow pan using the slide base and going up and down] {Voiceover} Enter, Project Talos. An open source, portable, video object recognition software tool for controlling multiple, camera-holding robots. 

Shot 3B:	[quick pan to unc filming the same professor from a different angle] Talos uses a central Machine Learning based “Director” which controls each camera based on their video stream to improve production value, and keep the audience engaged.

Shot 2B:	[transition from poor quality video of 2A into good quality video] With luck, we can lower the barrier for entry into simple, well produced, lecture content for all! Check out the open source Talos Project, hosted on github, today!
